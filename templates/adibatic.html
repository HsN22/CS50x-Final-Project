Calculate for an Adibatic system, the final Specific Enthalpy for Saturated Steam, given Pressure in bar and final Temperature in celcius

For an Adibatic system, the Entropy \( H \) is constant throught the process. This means it will be the same at the starting Pressure and the final Temperature.

Values of f g and fg are obtained from the Saturated tables

Steps:

1. Calculate the Dryness Fraction, \( x_{2} \) at \( s_2 \). Since it is Saturated steam, \( s_1 \) = \( s_g \)

2. Since process is Adibatic, \( s_{1} \) is the same as \( s_{2} \) so \( x_2 \) at \( s_2 \) can be calculated

3. Desired property, i.e final Enthalpy, \( h_2 \), can be calculated using \( x_2 \)

Specific Entropy: \[ s_1 = s_g \] \[ s_2 = s_f + x_2 s_{fg} \]
Specific Enthalpy: \[ h_2 = h_f + x_2 h_{fg} \]
